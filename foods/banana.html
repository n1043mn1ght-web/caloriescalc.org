Is Banana Good for Weight Loss?

At 89 kcal per 100g, bananas are a moderate-calorie fruit often avoided by dieters due to their sugar content — but the evidence does not support this fear. A medium banana provides 3.1g of dietary fiber, which promotes satiety and slows digestion. Studies consistently show that higher fiber intake is associated with lower body weight over time.

The key is context: a banana at 105 kcal is a far better snack than a granola bar at 200–400 kcal. Within a calorie deficit, bananas fit comfortably for most people. Use the calorie deficit calculator to find your daily target.

Glycemic Index and Blood Sugar

The glycemic index (GI) of bananas varies with ripeness. Unripe (green) bananas have a GI of approximately 30 — low. Ripe yellow bananas reach GI ~51 (medium). Overripe bananas can reach GI 65+. For blood sugar management, slightly underripe bananas are the better choice, and pairing with protein or fat further slows the glucose response.

Bananas as Pre-Workout Fuel

A medium banana eaten 20–30 minutes before training provides ~27g of fast-release carbohydrates that peak in the bloodstream right when energy demand is highest. Combined with 422mg of potassium for muscle function, this makes bananas one of the most practical and cost-effective pre-workout snacks available.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many calories in a banana?
A medium banana (118g) contains 105 calories. Per 100g, bananas have 89 calories. A small banana (81g) has 72 kcal and a large banana (136g) has 121 kcal. Use the slider above to calculate calories for any weight.
Is banana good for weight loss?
Yes, when eaten in moderation. At 105 kcal per medium banana, they are filling due to 3.1g of fiber and replace higher-calorie processed snacks well. One banana per day fits comfortably into most calorie-deficit diets.
Is banana keto?
No. A medium banana contains approximately 25g of net carbs (total carbs minus fiber), which would use up most of a typical keto daily limit of 20–50g. Bananas are not suitable for strict ketogenic diets.
Is banana high in sugar?
A medium banana contains about 14.4g of natural sugars. While moderate, these are naturally occurring fructose, glucose, and sucrose — unlike added sugars in processed foods. The fiber content slows their absorption, giving a medium glycemic index of ~51.
Can diabetics eat bananas?
In moderation. Bananas have a medium GI (~51). Choosing less ripe bananas lowers the GI significantly. Pairing with protein or fat further slows glucose response. Portion control (half a banana) is a practical approach. Always consult your doctor or dietitian for personalised advice.
How many bananas can I eat per day?
One to two medium bananas per day is a common amount that fits well within typical calorie and carbohydrate budgets. For people tracking macros, two medium bananas provide ~54g of carbohydrates and 210 calories — account for this in your daily intake.
