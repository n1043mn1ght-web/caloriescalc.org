Are Bananas Good for Weight Loss?

Bananas are often avoided by dieters due to their sugar content, but at 89 kcal per 100g they are actually lower in calories than many snacks. A medium banana provides significant fiber (3.1g), which increases satiety, and potassium (422mg), which supports muscle function.

The key question is not whether bananas are "high calorie" but whether they fit within your daily calorie budget. A medium banana at 105 kcal is a filling, nutritious snack that takes most people 15 minutes of walking to burn off.

Glycemic Index of Bananas

The glycemic index (GI) of bananas varies significantly with ripeness. Unripe (green) bananas have a GI of approximately 30 — considered low. Ripe yellow bananas have a GI around 51 (medium). Overripe bananas reach GI 65+. For blood sugar management, choosing slightly underripe bananas is beneficial.

Banana vs Other Fruits — Calorie Comparison

Per 100g: banana (89 kcal) vs apple (52 kcal) vs orange (47 kcal) vs grapes (69 kcal) vs strawberries (32 kcal). Bananas are higher in calories than most fruits primarily because of their lower water content and higher carbohydrate density — not because they are uniquely fattening.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many calories in a banana?
A medium banana (118g) contains 105 calories. Per 100g, bananas contain 89 calories. A small banana (81g) has 72 calories and a large banana (136g) has 121 calories.
How many carbs are in a banana?
A medium banana contains 27g of carbohydrates, of which 14.4g are sugar and 3.1g are dietary fiber. The net carbs (total minus fiber) are approximately 24g per medium banana.
Are bananas good for you?
Yes. Bananas provide potassium (422mg per medium banana — 9% of daily value), vitamin B6 (0.4mg — 25% DV), vitamin C (10mg — 11% DV), magnesium, and fiber. They are a convenient whole food that fits into most healthy diets.
Can I eat bananas on a diet?
Yes. At 105 kcal per medium banana, they fit into a calorie deficit when accounted for. Their fiber and potassium content make them a better choice than most processed snacks of similar calorie count. The key is tracking them accurately in your daily intake.
How many bananas can I eat per day?
There is no specific limit for healthy adults. One to two bananas per day is a common amount that fits well within typical daily calorie and carbohydrate budgets. People monitoring blood sugar may prefer limiting to one per day and choosing less ripe bananas.
